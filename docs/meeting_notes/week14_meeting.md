- [Member 4]: "Critical AI usage - learned to validate, not blindly trust"
- [Member 1]: "Project management - coordinating 4 people across 12 weeks"

---

**Challenges Faced:**

**Technical Challenges:**
1. **Normalization Trade-offs:** Deciding whether to keep TotalCost
   - **Resolution:** Thorough documentation of business rationale
   
2. **Seasonal Classification:** Seasons don't directly link to Bookings
   - **Resolution:** CASE statement with MONTH() function
   
3. **Occupancy Calculation:** True occupancy needs daily tracking
   - **Resolution:** Simplified metric with documented limitations

**Team Challenges:**
1. **Scheduling Conflicts:** Finding meeting times with 4 people
   - **Resolution:** Flexible Zoom + in-person hybrid meetings
   
2. **Skill Imbalance:** [Member 2] stronger in SQL initially
   - **Resolution:** Knowledge sharing, pair programming sessions
   
3. **Time Management:** Balancing with other courses
   - **Resolution:** Started early, built buffer time

**AI-Related Challenges:**
1. **Over-reliance Temptation:** Easy to accept AI outputs without checking
   - **Resolution:** Mandatory review process, log all corrections
   
2. **Context Gaps:** AI lacks business/economic domain knowledge
   - **Resolution:** Human expertise in economic interpretation
   
3. **Error Detection:** Some AI errors subtle (transitive dependencies)
   - **Resolution:** Peer review, cross-validation

---

**Lessons Learned:**

1. **Start Early:** Week 3 kickoff gave us buffer for challenges
2. **Document Everything:** Meeting notes, AI logs = saved us multiple times
3. **Test Thoroughly:** Catching errors early easier than fixing later
4. **Communicate Often:** Weekly meetings + Slack = team alignment
5. **Justify Decisions:** Denormalization OK if you explain why
6. **AI is Tool, Not Solution:** Accelerates but doesn't replace thinking
7. **Economic Context Matters:** Data without interpretation is just numbers
8. **Teamwork Makes It Better:** Solo project would be much harder
